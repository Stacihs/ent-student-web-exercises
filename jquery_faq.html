10 Facts About U.S. National Parks

How many parks are there?
63.
Do all 50 states have one?
No. Thirty states and two U.S. territories have one.
Can a state have more than one?
Yes.
When was the first one established?
1872, Yellowstone.
Which one is the largest park?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, in Alaska, at 13.2 million acres.
Which one is the smallest?
Gateway Arch National Park, Missouri, at 91 acres.
What is the northernmost park?
Gates of the Arctic National Park & Preserve, Alaska.
Which one is the most southern park?
Dry Tortugas National Park, Florida.
Which one has the most annual visitors?
Great Smoky Mountains National Park, North Carolina and Tennessee
When was the National Park Service that oversees the parks created?
1916.

Grand Teton National Park

  • Located in Wyoming.
  • Became a national park in 1929.
  • Named for the tallest mountain, Grand Teton, in the Teton Range.
  • Species of flora and fauna that have existed since prehistoric times can still be found here.

Yosemite National Park

  • Bordered by Sierra and Stanislaus National Forests.
  • Native Americans inhabited region for almost 4000 years.
  • Has 4 million visitors a year.
  • 95 percent of the park is designated wilderness.

Zion National Park

  • This was Utah's first national park.
  • Elevations range from 3600 to 8700 feet.
  • Total rainfall is only 15 inches a year.
  • Human habitation started here 8000 years ago.
