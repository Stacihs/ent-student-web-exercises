Grand Teton National Park

  • Located in Wyoming.
  • Became a national park in 1929.
  • Named for the tallest mountain, Grand Teton, in the Teton Range.
  • Species of flora and fauna that have existed since prehistoric times can still be found here.

Yosemite National Park

  • Bordered by Sierra and Stanislaus National Forests.
  • Native Americans inhabited region for almost 4000 years.
  • Has 4 million visitors a year.
  • 95 percent of the park is designated wilderness.

Zion National Park

  • This was Utah's first national park.
  • Elevations range from 3600 to 8700 feet.
  • Total rainfall is only 15 inches a year.
  • Human habitation started here 8000 years ago.
